Method notes

The interval sessions are built as broken threshold reps (800 m repeats, broken miles, 600 m, pyramids) — exactly the method's logic: chop the work into intervals to pile up the most volume at threshold pace without tipping over.

The mountain hike keeps heart rate low as a genuine easy day, while the 15 % treadmill session is really hill / strength work — about 480 m of vertical in 40 minutes at low heart rate. Hill work is a recognized part of the Bakken setup, so it slots in alongside the threshold days.

One thing I'm watching: the method is really lactate-controlled. Heart rate (80–85 %) is a solid proxy, but a lactate meter targeting ~2–4 mmol/L is the next step for the precision the system is built on. Double-threshold days — two sessions in one day — are the classic structure I haven't started yet.
